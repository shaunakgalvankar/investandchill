Opportunities Beyond Pandemic 2020

  • Post author By Invest & Chill Community, India
  • Post date September 21, 2020
  • No Comments on Opportunities Beyond Pandemic 2020

The Covid-19 Pandemic has been the most unprecedented event in recent times. But the future might be of growth, the impending global recession has made us collate for you the major sunrise sectors which can help us ride the tide.

The rise in Domestic Market economy: 

Sectors Expected to Thrive Post-COVID-19

1) E-Commerce retailers : Online is booming. Across the country and the globe, humans have been forced to use it for everything. The adoption rate in India has grown tremendously and will probably never retreat to pre-COVID levels. Indians have now been moving to e-commerce not as a luxury but necessity, Thanks to the cheap data provided to us by Mota Bhai that today the brands like Amazon, Jio Mart, Tata Cliq have been creating value not only for the customers for its various stakeholders too. 

2) Information Technology : The benefits of not relying on humans has never been more evident than during this pandemic. Before COVID-19, the rationale behind leveraging artificial intelligence (AI) was primarily based upon rising wage concerns and lack of available workers. Now high unemployment means an abundance of available workers, but working environments incorporating social distancing norms will require a transformation operations process to establish an efficient way of data processing and analytics with automation being the key driver. Wondered why the likes of TCS, Infosys, Info edge, Wipro have been on a winning streak???

3) Telecom : With more people working from home, the speed of our connections will be paramount to many of the growth sectors listed here. It can’t happen without faster connections and more homeowners are looking for opportunities to increase or accelerate their bandwidth as both parents and children are working and schooling from home. Here the reason why telecom has become the talk of the town with the feared Duopoly around the corner. The trio of VI, Jio, and Airtel have been able to dominate the traditional leaders like BSNL and MTNL which were owned by the state. 

4) Cloud kitchens : These are virtual restaurants without tables. With so many restaurants solely reliant upon home delivery of food, and the cost of maintaining a physical retail location and staff to operate a restaurant growing, the “dark” kitchen model — almost the e-commerce of food — will continue to grow. With tech-savvy players like Zomato, Swiggy, and Dunzo getting neck to neck it is going to be a boost to the traditional fast food players like Jubilant Food work of Dominoes, Hardcastle India of Mc Donalds, and a lot more. Hit Points: Holding companies like Info edge, Jubilant Food works, West life development fo Mc Donalds.

5) Health Care : With every country facing an issue to sustain its medical infrastructure the contingency has highlighted the importance of serious measures for the Medical and Pharma sector. As per agencies in the post-COVID-19 world, the industry would be provided exponential reinforcements by the governments to make it ready to face the worse. Hit Points: Pharma and Healthcare companies

6) E-Learning : With most of the children forced to continue their education from home, e-learning has had a tremendous boost. The same is true with colleges and universities. What benefit do large college campuses have for a learning environment which can be replicated, or perhaps even improved upon, by taking the classroom out of the equation? With college tuition outpacing inflation significantly over the last decade, those institutions will be marginalized quickly by e-learning platforms and the younger generation of teachers will excel in this exciting field. Hit Points: Byjus, White Hat Jr, Khan Academy.

The Fall of the Genius fund: Strategy, Exposure and Effects.

  • Post author By Invest & Chill Community, India
  • Post date September 21, 2020
  • No Comments on The Fall of the Genius fund: Strategy, Exposure and Effects.

LTCM was a Hedge Fund based in Greenwich, Connecticut,

It was founded by Solomon Brothers Bond trader John Meriwether and Nobel Price winning Economists Myron Scholes and Robert Merton. Due to the reputation of the owners it was also called as the Genius fund, as it had the perfect combination of Academicians and the Market maker.

The Dream Teams strategy earned a return of up to 42.8 % a year in 1995 followed by a 40% return in year 1996.

The company went bust in end of 1997 and was bailed by the Federal reserve,

Let’s see what happened.

Assumptions and Strategies used:

  • Efficient Market hypothesis: it states market at any point of time reflects all the publicly available information, this helped them to assume that a statistical theory can be applied to out perform market as there won’t be sudden shocks.
  • Riding the Losses to turn them into Gain: A small amount of losses were taken awaiting an opportunity for exponential gains.
  • Mean reversion theory: Values deviating away from the mean will reverse and converge to the mean, thus short position on overvalued security along with a long position on the undervalued one will provide to earn from the spread due to convergence.

Assets used:

  • US, Japan & Euro Sovereign bonds.
  • Euro Sovereign convergence bonds.
  • Out of the run and In the run US bonds.

What Happened in 1997?

  • Asian economic Crisis: With the Thai Baht crash due to the scarcity of foreign currency Asian economies like Thailand, Indonesia and Hong Kong were majorly affected, causing a chain effect reaching the Japan and mainland China.
  • Russian Economic Crisis: The domestic weakness of then Russia, led to it defaulting on its debt repayments and ended up devaluating its currency which had a ripple effect in most of the European economies.

Effect on the fund!!!

  • With LTCM Geographically exposed to the sovereign risk of Asian as well as Russian markets was facing an issue with the Global Sell off.
  • The fund was on a Leverage of 25x which was the main lag on its liquidity timeframe, With the volatility gripping in the fund went bust.
  • Due to its Sheer size and Reputation it was bailed out by the Federal Reserve in 1998.

What went wrong??

  • Theoretical Assumptions which went south in real market.
  • A High leverage of 25X which choked the management for liquidity.
  • Negligence of the stake holders and the regulators, to control the optimism of the management investors.

Learning: Everyone is beatable in the financial markets, leverage is a two-sided sword even for the Best.

How are Stocks picked: A Scientific Art!

  • No Comments on How are Stocks picked: A Scientific Art!

Ever wondered how to choose the best stocks sitting at home with limited to no research expertise and rounds of unsolicited tips going around in the society?

We are giving out a quick hack to find out the so-called best bets in the market

The Scientific Method:

With one of the best research houses and world class expertise the mutual fund and alternative fund industry has been able to find out a bucket of the best of businesses for their clients. The Science is to understand the logic behind this pickings lets find out how :

  • Find out the best performing equity fund/Alternative fund manager in the market.
  • Find out the fund managed by the best.
  • Make a list of the top 10 holdings of the fund.
  • Notice the Industry weightage of a company to the entire portfolio.
  • List the stocks in an excel sheet 1.

The Art Method:

  • Every company has a story a brand which builds a moat around it and makes to big to fail, The best of the best number crunchers fall in front of a visionary brand which aims to provide the best customer experience. The Art is to find the story and get a logic behind it,lets see how:
  • Try to find out the receipts of your house expenditure for last 5 months (Grocery bills, Bank Reciepts, Electricity Bill, Entertainment and Dining Bills)
  • List out the Number of repeated expenses towards a single brand, do keep in mind to find out if any brands changed/replaced with the time passed.
  • List out the brands and find out the reason they reach your house every month.
  • List down the brand as per their weightage in the Bill and find their listed entities.
  • Input the stocks in excel sheet 2.

Voila its done:

Find the commons from the first and the second excel sheet and you have your portfolio backed by the best of the scientific research possible and the its unique customer centric story.

Next time anyone asks about your portfolio tell him a Beautiful story backed by the numbers and stay put.
